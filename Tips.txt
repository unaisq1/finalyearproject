Bananas are berries, but strawberries aren't. Botanically, berries must have seeds and pulp and come from a single ovary—bananas fit the bill, strawberries don’t.

Octopuses have three hearts and their blood is blue due to a copper-rich protein called hemocyanin.

Wombat poop is cube-shaped, which helps keep it from rolling away and is used to mark territory.

Sharks predate trees. Sharks have existed for around 400 million years; trees appeared about 350 million years ago.

There's a planet made of diamonds. 55 Cancri e is believed to have a carbon-rich composition that could make it diamond-like.

You can smell rain. That scent is called petrichor, caused by oils and compounds released from the ground when it rains.

Butterflies can taste with their feet, which helps them identify whether a leaf is a good place to lay eggs.

Sloths can hold their breath longer than dolphins—up to 40 minutes by slowing their heart rate.

A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus. It rotates on its axis very slowly—once every 243 Earth days.

The Eiffel Tower can grow over 6 inches taller in summer due to thermal expansion of the metal.

The majority of dust in your home is made of dead skin cells and fabric fibers.

Some turtles can breathe through their butts—a process called cloacal respiration.

There's a species of jellyfish that is biologically immortal, Turritopsis dohrnii, which can revert to its juvenile state.

Your brain uses about the same amount of power as a 10-watt light bulb, even when you're asleep.

Honey never spoils. Archaeologists have found pots of honey in ancient Egyptian tombs that are still edible.

A bolt of lightning is five times hotter than the surface of the sun—about 30,000 Kelvin (53,540°F).

Scotland has 421 words for “snow”, including sneesl (to begin to rain or snow) and skelf (a large snowflake).

Octopus arms can think for themselves—two-thirds of an octopus's neurons are located in its arms, not its brain.

The shortest war in history was between Britain and Zanzibar in 1896. It lasted just 38–45 minutes.

The human stomach gets a new lining every 3 to 4 days to prevent it from digesting itself.
